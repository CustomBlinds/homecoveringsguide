Why safety latches suit Sedgefield homes

With a slower pace, many Sedgefield owners prefer open windows and natural airflow rather than always closing everything. Safety latches help you:

Working with shutters, screens and blinds

Latches are most useful when they are part of a small system:

  • Latches behind shutters so windows stay fixed in a safe position while blades manage light.
  • Latches plus fly screens to keep insects out while the gap is fixed.
  • Latches on small doors combined with security screens or gates on larger openings.

Questions Sedgefield owners often ask

Can latches be fitted to my existing aluminium or timber frames?

In most cases yes. Installers can mount latches to aluminium or timber as long as the frame is sound and there are suitable fixing points. Photos help them decide on position and screw type.

Do safety latches replace other security measures?

Latches are an extra control on how far a window or door can open, which helps with comfort and basic deterrence. They do not replace alarms, beams or other security layers on easily reached ground floor openings.

Use safety latches as part of a broader plan that includes good habits, screens, shutters and alarms where appropriate. Always follow the installation and operating guidance from your chosen supplier.
