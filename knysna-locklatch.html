Types of safety latches you will see

Different latch formats suit different openings and lifestyles. A typical range looks like this:

  • LockLatch® – general-purpose latch for doors and windows, ideal for night ventilation and extra security.
  • MiniLatch® – compact version for smaller windows or where you want only a narrow opening.
  • PetLatch® – for families that want controlled pet access without a permanent pet door.

In most Knysna homes, a mix of these latches is used: standard LockLatch for bedrooms and living spaces, MiniLatch for bathroom and side windows, and PetLatch for doors or windows used by cats and small dogs.

How installation usually works

  1. Assessment – the installer checks frame material (aluminium, timber, steel or uPVC), window type and opening sizes.
  2. Placement – ideal fixing points are chosen so the latch is strong, practical and discreet.
  3. Fitting – stainless-steel components are drilled and fixed to frame and sash, and the opening length is set.
  4. Testing – windows and doors are opened, locked and pulled against the latch to confirm security and smooth operation.
  5. Care tips – you are shown how to adjust the opening length and keep the latch clean in coastal conditions.

For most standard windows, installation is quick and can be combined with other work such as safety bars, security screens or new blinds.

Frequently asked questions about safety latches in Knysna

Will a safety latch stop baboons and monkeys?

It is designed to restrict the opening to a controlled gap and uses strong stainless-steel components. When combined with sensible window placement and, where needed, safety bars or screens, it significantly reduces the chance of baboons or monkeys pushing their way inside.

Can children still open the window further?

The latch creates a fixed maximum opening. Children can usually operate the normal window handle, but they cannot widen the gap beyond what you and your installer set, which reduces fall risk from upper-storey windows.

Does it work with aluminium and uPVC frames?

Yes. LockLatch-style latches are designed to fit aluminium, timber, steel and uPVC frames. The installer will use appropriate fasteners for each material so the fitting remains secure in coastal conditions.

What maintenance is needed in a coastal climate?

Basic care is enough: occasional rinsing with fresh water to remove salt, a mild detergent if needed, and checking screws once or twice a year. Stainless-steel components are selected specifically for Garden Route environments.

Can I add these to a home that already has blinds or shutters?

Yes. Safety latches can be retrofitted to most existing windows and doors. In many homes, they are added alongside existing blinds, shutters and safety bars to upgrade security without changing the whole window treatment.
