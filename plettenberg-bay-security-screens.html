What security screens are designed to do

Security screens use stronger mesh and frames than normal fly screens. They are designed to resist pushing, pulling and impact while still looking light and letting air through.

  • Keep insects out and slow down forced entry
  • Help deter baboons and other wildlife from entering
  • Allow safe night ventilation in bedrooms and living areas

Where to use security screens in Plett

  • Sea-facing sliders that you like to leave open at night
  • Kitchen and scullery doors used constantly through the day
  • Bedrooms where you want airflow with the lights on

Many homes combine screens with safety bars or security shutters on the most exposed openings and use lighter options elsewhere.

Combining screens, bars and safety latches

True security is usually layered. In the Garden Route, a common strategy is:

  • Security screens on main living doors and sliders
  • Safety bars on vulnerable windows
  • Safety latches like LockLatch on selected openings for night ventilation

FAQ: Security screens in Plettenberg Bay

Do security screens look like burglar bars?
No. They look more like a fine mesh panel. From inside you mostly see the view through a darker layer rather than vertical bars.
Can security screens stop baboons?
They greatly reduce easy access but nothing is completely baboon proof. Screens should be part of a wider plan that includes latches and good habits.
Are these screens child safe?
Yes. They help prevent small children from falling through open doorways while still allowing airflow.
