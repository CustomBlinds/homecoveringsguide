Fly Screens in Mossel Bay

With warm weather and coastal air, Mossel Bay households often leave windows and doors open. Fly screens let you enjoy that airflow without mosquitos, moths and other insects joining supper.

Quick answers

  • Where: Bedrooms, kitchens, coastal-facing lounges and sliding doors.
  • Why: Keep insects out while allowing ventilation and light.
  • Types: Fixed screens, hinged screens for doors, and retractable cassette screens for stackers and french doors.

Types of fly screens for Mossel Bay homes

Fixed window screens

Fixed screens are discrete aluminium frames with mesh, fitted over opening sections. They work well for:

  • Bedrooms that need night-time ventilation.
  • Bathroom windows that are left tilted or open for steam.
  • Kitchen windows near sinks where insects are a problem.

Hinged or sliding door screens

Hinged screen doors sit in front of existing doors so you can open the solid door and keep the screen closed. Sliding screen panels can be paired with large patio sliders.

Retractable cassette fly screens

Retractable systems disappear into a cassette when not in use and pull across when needed. They suit:

  • Stacking doors leading to decks or braai areas.
  • Balcony doors where you don’t want a permanent frame in the way.

Mesh choices and coastal considerations

In coastal air, ask specifically about corrosion resistance and mesh options. Aluminium and stainless fixings outperform untreated steel over time.
  • Standard mesh: Fine enough to block mosquitos, still lets light in.
  • Pet or security mesh: Heavier mesh that resists claws and bumps, sometimes combined with security-grade framing.
  • Cleaning: Screens near the sea benefit from gentle washing to remove salt and dust.

Fly screens FAQ for Mossel Bay

Do fly screens block my view?

Standard insect mesh is designed to be as invisible as possible from a short distance. Darker mesh usually “disappears” more than light mesh when you look through it.

Can I add fly screens to existing timber or aluminium windows?

In most cases, yes. A site visit will confirm where frames can be fixed without interfering with opening mechanisms.

Will fly screens help with monkeys or baboons?

Standard mesh is not designed for baboons or strong animals. In those cases, consider security screens or security shutters instead of simple insect mesh.
