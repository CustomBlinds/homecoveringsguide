Why Sedgefield owners consider security screens

Sedgefield has a mix of permanent residents, semi retired owners and holiday visitors. Many owners want:

  • Security that still looks light and coastal, not like heavy bars.
  • Night ventilation with windows or doors partially open.
  • Clear views toward the lagoon, dunes or forest during the day.

How security screens differ from fly screens

Security screens use stronger mesh, reinforced frames and proper locking systems. They are part of a security plan, while standard fly screens are only for insects.

  • Security mesh resists cutting and impact better than normal insect mesh.
  • Frames are designed for strength and fixed more securely.
  • Locks and hinges are chosen to resist forced entry.

Good locations for security screens in Sedgefield

  • Patio doors and sliders that open to verandas and braai spaces.
  • Side entrances and scullery doors that are often left ajar.
  • Ground floor bedroom windows that you would like to vent at night.

Combining security screens with shutters and latches

Security screens work best as part of a layered approach:

  • Screens plus security shutters on key areas for deeper security and light control.
  • Screens plus outdoor blinds on decks to manage weather while screens manage security.
  • Screens plus safety latches on selected windows for controlled opening sizes.

Questions Sedgefield owners often ask

Are security screens enough on their own?

On many doors and some windows, a security screen can be a primary barrier. In higher risk situations you may still choose to combine screens with shutters, alarms or beams. It is better to think of screens as one part of a full security plan.

Will security screens make my home feel closed in?

Dark mesh tends to read like mild tint rather than a heavy structure. You keep airflow and day time views, especially on lagoon or garden facing openings, while gaining a sense of security at night or when you are away.
