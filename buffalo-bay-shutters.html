Which shutter materials work at the coast

Moist sea air and strong sun can be hard on timber. Most Buffalo Bay homes focus on:

  • PVC interior shutters for wet rooms, sea-facing bedrooms and general light control.
  • Aluminium security shutters where you want privacy and security in one product.

PVC interior shutters offer a clean painted look and are easy to wipe down. Aluminium shutters suit doors that open to decks or paths where you want strong locking and airflow at night.

Shutters are useful because they control more than light:

  • A neat alternative to heavy burglar bars in main living spaces.

Pairing shutters with screens and safety latches

For coastal homes, shutters are often part of a layered approach:

  • Fly screens to keep insects out when windows are open behind shutters.
  • Security screens for sliding doors where you want full view and airflow without traditional bars.
  • Safety latches such as LockLatch to hold windows partially open at night while remaining locked.

Practical questions from Buffalo Bay owners

Do shutters work on angled or irregular openings?

Many suppliers can handle arches and special shapes, but these need more detailed measuring and planning. Share clear photos and dimensions early so you know what is practical before you fall in love with a concept.

Can I use shutters instead of burglar bars on all openings?

Security shutters can replace bars on many windows and doors. For very wide openings or upper decks, you might still combine them with security screens or other measures. Always discuss this with a security-aware installer that understands your risk profile.
