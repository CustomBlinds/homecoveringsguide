Shadeports in Mossel Bay

Shadeports protect cars, boats and caravans from sun and hail while still allowing air to move around the structure. In Mossel Bay, they are commonly used for open parking bays and driveways where a full garage is not practical.

Quick guide for Mossel Bay shadeports

  • Uses: Carports, boat and trailer parking, extra guest parking, work areas.
  • Climate: Designed for sun and rain, but check wind design for exposed coastal sites.
  • Cover options: Knitted shade cloth or solid sheeting depending on the goal.

Why Mossel Bay properties use shadeports

Along the Garden Route, vehicles are often parked outside because of limited garage space or additional family cars. Shadeports help with:

  • Reducing cabin temperatures and UV damage to interiors.
  • Helping protect paintwork from long term sun exposure.
  • Providing a defined parking or work area without major building work.

Design choices for coastal shadeports

When designing a shadeport for coastal Mossel Bay, prioritise corrosion-resistant materials and proper wind engineering for exposed sites.
  • Support structure: Galvanised or coated steel suited to coastal exposure.
  • Covers: Shade cloth for breathability, or solid sheeting where more rain protection is required.
  • Orientation: The direction of sun and prevailing wind over the parking area.
  • Wind design: Exposed coastal sites need proper engineering certification.

Shadeports FAQ for Mossel Bay

What size shadeport do I need for two cars?

A standard double shadeport is typically 6m wide by 5.5m deep. If you have larger vehicles or want extra space for storage, consider increasing the depth to 6m or more.

Will a shadeport survive coastal wind?

When properly engineered and installed, yes. Exposed sites near the coast require calculations for local wind loads and proper foundation work. Always discuss site conditions with your installer.

Shade cloth or solid sheeting – which is better?

Shade cloth allows air and some light through, reducing wind load and heat buildup. Solid sheeting offers better rain protection but needs stronger frames. Your choice depends on whether you prioritise ventilation or weather protection.
