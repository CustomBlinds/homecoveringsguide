Made to measure blinds online in South Africa

Made-to-measure means each blind is cut to your window rather than picked off a shelf in fixed sizes. Ordered online, that removes the light gaps and overhangs of ready-made blinds, but it also means a wrong measurement is your risk, not the shop's, unless the seller offers a genuine fit guarantee. This guide explains how the made-to-measure online process works in South Africa and what to check before you commit.

How to judge any online blind seller in South Africa

Before ordering blinds from any platform, run it against these five questions. They are the ones that decide whether you are satisfied a year later.

  • Made-to-measure, not fixed sizes. Each blind should be cut to your exact width and drop, not chosen from standard widths that leave gaps.
  • Manufactured in South Africa. Locally made blinds arrive faster, can be remade quickly if needed, and the supplier is accountable under South African consumer law.
  • A real fit guarantee. Because a custom blind cannot simply be returned, the seller should cover a measuring slip: a trim if it is too big, a discount or remake if it is too small.
  • Live, transparent pricing. You should see the price the moment you enter your sizes, not wait on a quote that arrives by email.
  • Real human support. Someone should answer when a question or a problem comes up, before and after the sale.
