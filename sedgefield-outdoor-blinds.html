Sedgefield climate and how outdoor blinds help

Sedgefield can be calm one moment and windy the next, especially when weather moves along the coast. There is also bright low sun across the lagoon and dunes. Outdoor blinds give you the ability to:

  • Block wind from one or two exposed sides without closing the deck completely.
  • Soften glare on long summer evenings while you eat outside.
  • Protect furniture from salt air and sideways rain in winter.

Blind types that work in Sedgefield

Clear PVC blinds for view decks

Clear PVC blinds are popular where the lagoon or dune view is the main feature. They keep decks usable in marginal weather and suit family homes and holiday cottages.

Mesh outdoor blinds for sun and privacy

Mesh blinds are useful on village side houses and north or west facades that take strong sun. They cool the space, reduce glare and add daytime privacy from neighbours and passing traffic.

Many Sedgefield owners combine clear PVC facing the best view with mesh on the sunniest or most overlooked sides.

Planning measurements and fixing points

When you request quotes, good information saves time:

  1. Measure the width of each opening at top, middle and bottom and note the smallest size.
  2. Measure height from finished floor to underside of the beam or lintel.
  3. Photograph the deck from inside and from the garden or driveway.
  4. Note which side normally takes the strongest wind and how often doors and sliders are used.

Combining outdoor blinds with awnings and shutters

The most comfortable Sedgefield outdoor rooms use a small combination of products:

  • A roof or awning for overhead sun and rain.
  • Outdoor blinds for side wind and glare.
  • Interior shutters in bedrooms and lounges for privacy when blinds are open.

Questions Sedgefield owners often ask

Will outdoor blinds make my deck too hot in summer?

Fully closed PVC blinds can trap heat on very still days. A mix of mesh and PVC, or leaving a small gap open, usually keeps comfort levels good while still stopping wind and mist.

Are outdoor blinds suitable for guest houses and rentals?

Yes, if they are specified correctly and guests are shown a simple operating routine. They can extend breakfast or sundowner seating and reduce weather related complaints in season.
