What safety latches are designed to solve

Safety latches hold a window or door in a partially open, lockable position. They are small fittings with a big effect on comfort and safety in coastal homes.

  • Night ventilation while keeping frames locked
  • Limiting how far children can open windows
  • Helping pets enjoy air without escaping
  • Slowing opportunistic entry through open gaps

Where Plett homes use LockLatch-type devices

  • Bedroom doors and windows facing decks or gardens
  • Kitchen and scullery windows that stand open for long periods
  • Upstairs windows where falls are a concern
  • Holiday homes left closed up between visits

Combining safety latches with screens and bars

Latches work best as part of a bigger plan. In the Garden Route they are often paired with security screens and safety bars so that airflow is controlled and barriers line up.

FAQ: LockLatch in Plettenberg Bay

Can LockLatch keep baboons out?
It restricts how far a window or door can open, which helps deter baboons and other animals. It should be used together with good habits and, where needed, screens or bars.
Does it fit aluminium and wooden frames?
Yes. Systems like LockLatch are designed for aluminium, timber and uPVC frames. Installation technique and hardware will differ by material.
Is this only for bedrooms?
No. Many owners use latches on living rooms, kitchens and even garage access doors where they want secure airflow without fully opening the entry.
