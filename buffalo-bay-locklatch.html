Why safety latches suit Buffalo Bay homes

Apart from safety and security, latches support comfort:

  • Night ventilation while you sleep with the sound of the sea.
  • Airflow in children’s rooms without the risk of large openings.
  • Fresh air in bathrooms and kitchens when you are away from home.

How latches integrate with shutters, screens and blinds

A latch is a small part of a broader system:

  • Use latches on windows behind shutters so you can angle blades for privacy while the opening stays restricted.
  • Combine latches with fly screens to stop insects while the gap remains fixed.
  • On doors, pair latches with security screens or gates to keep the main leaf locked while a smaller section provides airflow.

Buffalo Bay questions about safety latches

Can latches fit on existing aluminium and timber windows?

In most cases yes. Installers can attach latches to aluminium or timber frames, as long as there is enough space and the frame is sound. Share photos so they can confirm before you commit.

Are latches enough on their own for security?

A latch helps restrict opening size and acts as a deterrent, especially higher up where access is limited. For ground floor or easily reached windows you may still combine latches with security screens or shutters.
