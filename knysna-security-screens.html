Quick answers for busy people

  • What are security screens? High strength stainless-steel mesh set in structural aluminium frames that sit over doors and windows while keeping views and airflow.
  • Why Knysna homeowners use them: To leave sliders, patios and back doors open for air without relying on heavy bars or gates.
  • Where they work best: Bedrooms opening to decks, braai patios, sculleries, kitchen doors and any “in and out” slider towards the garden or pool.

What security screens actually do in a coastal town like Knysna

Knysna homes live with lagoon mist, salt air, strong afternoon wind and curious baboons on certain routes. Security screens are designed so that you can still slide open the doors for airflow while keeping a clean, secure barrier in place.

Instead of solid bars, the mesh is tensioned tightly in an aluminium frame. From inside, you still see out. From the street, people mostly see a dark, flat surface. The idea is less about “jail bars” and more about calm everyday living with open doors.

Security screens vs fly screens vs burglar bars

If you are just starting research, the terms can be confusing. This is the basic comparison many Knysna homeowners work through.

Security screens

  • Stainless-steel mesh and structural frames
  • Designed to resist forced entry and pet impact
  • Good for sliders, hinged doors and key bedroom windows
  • Higher cost, but long-term solution

Fly / insect screens

  • Lighter mesh with slim frames
  • Stops insects, not intruders
  • Perfect if security is already handled elsewhere
  • Often paired with outdoor blinds and shutters

Burglar bars & gates

  • Bars or solid gates fixed to frames
  • Strong, but visually intrusive and hard to remove later
  • Can make windows feel smaller and rooms darker
  • Harder to match with modern coastal interiors

Most Garden Route homes now mix solutions: for example, security screens on main sliders and bedroom doors, with lighter insect screens or outdoor blinds where the risk is lower.

Where security screens make the biggest difference in Knysna

In and around Knysna, you see security screens most often in these positions:

  • Stacking or sliding doors from living areas to decks and braai patios
  • Kitchen or scullery doors that stand open all day for breeze
  • Bedroom doors opening directly to a balcony or pool
  • Side passage doors that are hidden from the street but busy in daily life

The priority is usually the same: keep natural air and keep daily movement easy, but add a layer of protection so the house does not feel exposed when everything is open.

What to look for when comparing security screens

When you compare options or quotes, these details matter more than marketing names or technical jargon:

  • Mesh specification: coastal-capable stainless mesh that resists rust and stays flat under tension.
  • Frame and fixings: aluminium frames with properly anchored fixings into the surrounding structure, not just the timber or aluminium frame.
